I was tracking movies on Letterboxd, TV on Trakt, anime on MyAnimeList, books on Goodreads, games on Backloggd, music on Apple Music, football in OneFootball. Seven apps. Same routine. Different libraries that never talked to each other.

EyeCandy is what happens when you stop tolerating that.

Schedule

One calendar for every release.

Episode airs Tuesday. Manga chapter drops Friday. Game launches in 12 days. Match kicks off at 19:00. They all live in the same weekly view.

  • Pin specific dates or recurring days (every Thursday)
  • Per-item reminders, on your terms
  • Live football, basketball, and tennis fixtures pulled in automatically
  • Goal alerts for your starred teams
Discover

Search it. Swipe it. Spin for it.

Three ways to land on the next thing you watch, read, or play.

Universal search

One bar. Nine media types. Browse, filter by year and genre, sort by Top Rated, Trending, or Newest.

Swipe to decide

Tinder for what's next. Skip, queue, or mark as seen. Tap up for an instant 10/10.

Surprise me

734 titles in your library and no idea what to watch tonight. One tap, one answer.

Track

Real detail screens. Not placeholders.

Every poster opens to the same thing: real metadata, your rating, your status, a way to schedule it, a way to add it to a custom collection, and Candy on standby.

Collections

Make a list. Put anything in it.

Custom collections cross every category. "2026 media" can hold a Letterboxd-style log next to a manga next to a Premier League match next to an album. Same shelf.

  • Cover art auto-stitches from items inside
  • Long-press to delete, drag to reorder
  • Synced across devices the moment you sign in
Sports

Match days, in the same place as everything else.

Football, basketball, tennis. Live scores polled every 60 seconds during matches. League standings color-coded for Champions League, Europa, and relegation. Knockout brackets with leg aggregates and away-goals notes.

  • Star teams. Auto-schedule their fixtures
  • 15-minute kickoff alerts
  • Goal push notifications for starred sides
  • ATP and WTA rankings for tennis
Candy AI

An AI that's actually read your library.

Most chatbots guess. Candy already knows you rated The Departed an 8 and watched The Usual Suspects last quarter. It feeds your full library, ratings, schedule, and watchlist into every reply.

  • Snap a photo of a poster, scene, or album cover. Candy IDs it and links straight to the detail screen
  • Ask "schedule something for Friday" and it actually does it
  • Voice playback with multiple accents (Neural2 + WaveNet)
  • Free tier: 15 chats, 15 listens, 5 image IDs daily. Bring your own DeepSeek key for unlimited
Bring it all in

Import your years of history. Don't start over.

EyeCandy speaks the formats every other tracker uses. CSV from IMDb, XML from MAL, S3-key sync with Open Library, OAuth with TMDB, MAL, RAWG, and Apple Music.

Notifications

Quiet by default. Loud when you want them to be.

Episode-airing alerts. Chapter-release pings. Goal alerts for your starred teams. 15-minute kickoff reminders. Or none of the above. Every category toggles independently.

Stored on-device, scoped per signed-in account. Sign out and the next person on your phone sees a clean inbox.

Profile

Your library, in one number.

882 total. 352 rated. 7.3 average. Heatmap of every active day this year. Top genre. Longest streak. December gets a year-end recap built from the same data, no Spotify Wrapped tax.
